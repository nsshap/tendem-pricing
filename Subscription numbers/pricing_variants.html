Subscription model analysis · FP margin 27.5% · Data: March 2026

Reference costs (from real data): AI task cost: median $0.38, P80 $0.96 · HITL task cost: median $20.91, P80 $74.05
Tuned FP (27.5% margin): AI median $0.53, P80 $1.32 · HITL median $28.84, P80 $102.13
Margin formula: margin = 1 − multiplier × (1 − fp_margin) / (1 − discount). Margin does NOT depend on task complexity — only on multiplier & discount.

Effective value — purchasing power of the subscription: balance / (1 − discount). Shows how much a user would need to spend at full price (no subscription) to get the same number of tasks. E.g. Starter $100, 1.2×, 10% off → balance $120, but each task is 10% cheaper, so $120 stretches like $133 at full price.
Overage margin — our margin on tasks purchased after the balance is used up. User pays discounted FP (= FP × (1 − discount)), our cost = FP × (1 − fp_margin). Formula: 1 − (1 − fp_margin) / (1 − discount). This is always positive as long as discount < fp_margin (27.5%).
